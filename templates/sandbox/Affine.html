Affine Cipher Encryption and Decryption

The Affine cipher is a type of monoalphabetic substitution cipher. It encrypts a message by applying a simple mathematical function.

Encryption Formula: \( E(x) = (ax + b) \mod 26 \)

Decryption Formula: \( D(x) = a^{-1}(x - b) \mod 26 \)

Where:

  • \( E(x) \) is the encrypted letter
  • \( D(x) \) is the decrypted letter
  • \( x \) is the position of the letter in the alphabet (0-25)
  • \( a \) and \( b \) are the key parameters
  • \( a \) should be chosen to be relatively prime to 26 (i.e., they have no common factors except 1).
  • The whole process relies on working modulo 26 (the length of the English alphabet).
